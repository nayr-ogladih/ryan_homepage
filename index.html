Getting to know Ryan

Webpage first created by Ryan Hidalgo on Tuesday, March 7th 2023

I have always been fascinated with computers & technology growing up as a child. My first experience was when I played a very popular computer game called World of Warcraft. I would download built-in modifications for the game (sometimes I would have upwards to 20+ mods running at a time) to gain an edge and more user-playability.

My first hands-on experience with any type of coding would be fixing my default page on then most popular website at the time called Myspace. I would look up codes on how to fix the background of my profile page, customize my top friends section, have a dedicated song playing when a person visited my page, the list goes on.

Just to give a little background on who I am and where I come from. I am a first-generation Filipino American, born from asian immigrants coming from the Philipphines in the 1990's. I was born in Vista, California and raised in Oceanside. Being Filipino my parents had certain expectations of me - go to college and become a nurse or a doctor like everyone in my family.

Education

Suffice to say I did go to college to honor my parents, because they believed getting an education will lead to better life. I went against their wishes by not becoming a nurse and graduated in 2016 with a Bachelor's in Economics from California State University of San Marcos.

I took a school break after graduation to detox and gain mental stability. In 2018 I decided to study for the GRE (graduate record examination) up until the Coronavirus pandemic started.

In the year 2020 me and billion of lives were screeched to a halt and put in danger. I had to put my life on hold to help my family.

Why did I become a Software Engineer?

I Realized I did not want to go to school for potentially another five years, so I looked for alternatives.

  1. A good friend of mine from college recommended I look into coding.
  2. It is a alot of fun creating your own website or code a program.
  3. It can be a tool to help with my everday tasks.
  4. I was not the smartest person in the class, but I knew was very hard-working, dedicated to my craft, and disciplined. That is all someone needs to become a Software Engineer.

I just know that with time and persistence my portfolio will grow...

Past work experiences

My day job is managing a restaurant called Poki-Poki, sometimes I provide assistance to two other locations.

When I am not working, I am working on my dream of becoming a full-stack software engineer.
